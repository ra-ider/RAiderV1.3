What to do if you are struggling with mental health problems?



Progressive Muscle Relaxation.
Progressive muscle relaxation is a form of therapy that involves tightening and relaxing your muscle groups, one at a time, in a specific pattern. The goal is to release tension from your muscles, while helping you recognize what that tension feels like.





Mindful Breathing Exercise.
The breath is the life force. Focusing on the breath is particularly helpful because it serves as an anchor–something you can turn your attention to at any time if you start to feel stressed or carried away by negative emotions.





Tips for better sleep.
Getting enough sleep is essential for helping a person maintain optimal health and well-being. An insufficient amount of sleep can lead to serious repercussions. Sleep deprivation leaves people vulnerable to attention lapses, reduced cognition, delayed reactions, and mood shifts.





Tackle your worries.
Constant worrying can take a heavy toll. It can keep you up at night and make you tense and edgy during the day. Keeping worries to yourself only causes them to build up until they seem overwhelming. But saying them out loud can often help you to make sense of what you’re feeling and put things in perspective.





Reframe Unhelpful Thoughts.
Reframing challenges the negative thoughts and beliefs that contribute to distress. When negative thinking becomes regular, it might make it difficult for you to carry out daily tasks. For this reason, changing the way you think is essential for enhancing your wellbeing.





Take Control of Stress.
Achieving effective stress management enables to release the grip that stress has on your life and improves your ability to be happier, healthier, and more effective. The ultimate aim is a balanced life, with time for work, relationships, relaxation, and enjoyment, as well as the fortitude to withstand pressure and face obstacles head-on.





Social Connection.
Physical, mental, and emotional health all improve with social engagement. In addition to reducing anxiety and depression and assisting with mood regulation, it can boost empathy and self-esteem as well as our immune systems. We risk our health when we ignore our need to connect.





Healthy Lifestyle.
Healthy life is built on a foundation of a balanced diet, regular exercise, and enough sleep. Living a healthy lifestyle keeps you active, healthy, and less prone to illness. It is a way of living that helps you enjoy more aspects of your life.





Keep Learning.
Most of us find happiness in learning new topics that we are interested in, as well as in spending time perfecting passions we have. It makes sense that scheduling time for these activities in your daily life will increase your level of satisfaction overall.





Beditation – Simple Meditation to Help You Sleep.
Beditation could be the solution to our bedtime woes. It redirects our mind away from thinking to sensing and being and allows us to relax and drift off. Before going to bed, even ten minutes of mindfulness meditation can significantly improve level of relaxation and quality of sleep.
