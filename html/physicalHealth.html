Strengthening and Stretching for Rheumatoid Arthritis of the Hand



MCP Flexion
Hand and finger exercises can help strengthen your hands and fingers, increase your range of motion, and give you pain relief. Stretch only until you feel tightness. You shouldn't feel pain. Bend your knuckles into a table top position. Keep your wrist straight. Hold for 5 seconds. Repeat at least four times with each hand.





Tendon Gliding
These exercises allow each tendon to reach its greatest amount of movement. They also reduce hand swelling. Tendon-gliding exercises are as important to the hand as aerobic exercise is to the heart. Start with your fingers straight every time you do these exercise. Make each type of fist, one at a time, with your fingers. First hook fist then straight fist and finally full fist. Hold for 5 seconds. Repeat at least four times with each hand.





Finger Abduction
Try this stretch to help with pain relief and to improve the range of motion in your hands. Place your hand palm-down on a table or other flat surface. Gently straighten your fingers as flat as you can against the surface without forcing your joints. Hold for 30 to 60 seconds. Repeat at least four times with each hand.





Finger Radial Walking
This exercise is very beneficial for those who cannot move their fingers without feeling a sharp pain. Put your hand on a table, palm facing down. Slowly lift the index finger, move it towards the thumb, then place the finger down. Do the same with the middle, ring and pinky fingers. Repeat 2-3 times on each hand.





Wrist Circumduction
Stretching and exercising the muscles around your wrists will keep the wrists flexible and strong, and help you avoid repetitive motion and stress injuries. Begin by supporting your forearm with your opposite hand just below your wrist. You can rest your elbow on a table for added support .Keep your fingers in a loose fist. Using both arms roll your wrists in one direction, then the other. Rotate in circles as far as comfortably possible.





Gross Grip
This exercise can make it easier to open door knobs and hold things without dropping them. Hold a soft ball in your palm and squeeze it as hard as you can. Hold for few seconds and release. Repeat 10 to 15 times on each hand. Do this exercise two to three times a week, but rest your hands for 48 hours in between sessions. Don't do this exercise if your thumb joint is damaged.





Pinch Grip
This exercise helps strengthen the muscles of your fingers and thumb. It can help you turn keys, open food packages, and use the gas pump more easily. Pinch a soft foam ball between the tips of your fingers and your thumb. Hold for 30 to 60 seconds. Repeat 10 to 15 times on both hands. Finger Adduction
To improve the strength of the small muscles that help pull your fingers together, perform the finger adduction exercise. To do this, take a foam, and then squeeze the ball between your fingers. Hold the tension for a few seconds, and then release. Repeat the exercise 10 to 15 repetitions, and then move on to the next exercise.
